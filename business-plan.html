What We Do

We are an F&B consulting practice built specifically for senior living operators. Dining is the largest controllable cost in most senior living buildings — and the most neglected. We come in, identify the inefficiencies, and fix them.

Our target is an 8–12% improvement in overall dining operations for every client we take on — measured through food cost reduction, labor efficiency, resident satisfaction, and vendor savings.

The Core Pitch We pay for ourselves. The savings and improvements we identify in the first 90 days consistently exceed the cost of the engagement.

Why We Can Deliver It

The 8–12% improvement target isn't a marketing number — it's grounded in the specific experience we bring to every engagement. Most F&B consultants in this space come from one background. We come from all of them.

Restaurant Ownership & Management

We have owned and operated restaurants — which means we understand food service from the perspective of someone whose livelihood depends on getting it right. Real P&L accountability. Real hiring, firing, scheduling, and training under pressure. Real vendor negotiations where margins are thin and mistakes are expensive. That experience doesn't come from a textbook and it's immediately visible when we walk into a senior living kitchen.

Correctional Food Service

Correctional food service is one of the most demanding environments in institutional dining — high volume, strict per-meal cost controls, zero tolerance for waste, and rigid compliance requirements. You feed hundreds of people per day on some of the tightest budgets in institutional food service, with a workforce that requires constant oversight and training. The discipline, systems thinking, and cost control habits built in that environment are exactly what an underperforming senior living kitchen needs. Where others see complexity, we see a familiar problem we've already solved.

Institutional & Senior Living Operations

Beyond restaurant and correctional experience, we bring direct senior living F&B knowledge — the specific KPIs operators watch, the therapeutic diet requirements, the texture-modified programming, the survey exposure, and the vendor landscape that serves this market in Birmingham and the Southeast.

The Full Skillset

  • Restaurant ownership — P&L, hiring, vendor contracts, menu engineering
  • Correctional food service — high-volume, tight budgets, strict compliance
  • Senior living operations — KPIs, therapeutic diets, memory care nutrition
  • Labor scheduling and workforce management at scale
  • Vendor negotiation — demonstrated seven-figure savings
  • Kitchen production systems and recipe standardization
  • Staff training in high-turnover environments
  • GPO access and Birmingham-market distributor relationships

How the 8–12% Gets Realized

  • Food cost reduction — vendor renegotiation, portion standardization, waste reduction, smarter ordering
  • Labor efficiency — scheduling optimization, cross-training, reducing overtime
  • Resident satisfaction lift — menu quality improvements that reduce complaints and support census
  • Operational consistency — standardized recipes and production sheets that survive staff turnover
  • On the ground — we walk the kitchen, not just review spreadsheets remotely
  • Weekly accountability — formal written report keeps the team focused and ownership informed
