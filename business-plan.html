Executive Summary

Senior living operators across the country are sitting on one of the most controllable — and most neglected — cost centers in their buildings: food and beverage. Dining is typically the largest non-labor expense a facility carries, yet most operators lack the internal expertise to manage it well. The result is food cost overruns, low resident satisfaction, and high staff turnover in the kitchen.

This business plan outlines the launch of a focused F&B consulting practice serving senior living communities — assisted living, memory care, independent living, and skilled nursing — with an initial concentration in the Birmingham, Alabama metro area and a 150-mile regional radius.

We bring hands-on restaurant operations experience, institutional food service knowledge, and vendor relationships directly to the buildings that need them most. Our model is built around a weekly report-driven retainer — not a phone call, a formal deliverable — that gives operators visibility, accountability, and results without having to hire a full-time F&B director.

The Core Pitch We pay for ourselves. Vendor savings and food cost reductions identified in the first 90 days consistently exceed the cost of the engagement. Operators get results without adding headcount.

Market Opportunity

The Numbers

  • 30,000+ senior living communities in the U.S.
  • $22B+ annual food service spend across the sector
  • Baby Boomer demand outpacing new supply — development has slowed, acquisitions are rising
  • Private equity and investor groups are buying distressed assets and need immediate operational support post-acquisition
  • Most mid-size operators have no dedicated F&B expertise on staff

Why Now in Birmingham

  • Active acquisition market — multiple investor groups purchasing AL/MC assets in Central Alabama
  • New operators routinely underestimate dining complexity and need outside help fast
  • No dominant local F&B consulting presence
  • Established relationships with operators and vendors already in market
  • Proximity enables consistent on-site engagement — a real advantage over remote consultants

Go-to-Market Strategy

Phase 1 — Birmingham Metro (Now – Month 6)

Lead with the local market. Birmingham and the surrounding suburbs (Hoover, Vestavia Hills, Homewood, Trussville, Mountain Brook, Gardendale, Bessemer, Pelham) represent a concentrated target pool within 30 minutes of each other. On-site presence is a genuine differentiator over remote consultants.

Phase 2 — Referral Network Activation (Month 2–4)

Senior housing is relationship-driven. The most efficient path to new clients is through operators, investors, and real estate groups already in the ecosystem. One active referral partner — a director of operations, a regional VP, a healthcare architect — is worth more than 50 cold emails.

Active Referral Partner: Umbredo Living (Birmingham) Director of Operations at Umbredo Living — a startup senior housing acquisition company with 4 closed properties and active deal flow — has offered to make introductions to Jewish Orthodox SNF portfolio owners and carry our capabilities deck to the NIC Fall Conference in Chicago (October 2026). This is a high-value, warm-relationship channel to prioritize.

Phase 3 — NIC Fall Conference, Chicago (October 2026)

The National Investment Center for Seniors Housing & Care (NIC) Fall Conference is the premier industry gathering — every major operator, investor, and portfolio owner attends. "Does anyone know someone who can fix our dining?" is a recurring conversation. We need a polished one-page capabilities summary and pricing overview in hand by September 15 to distribute through our referral network at the event.

Operations Plan

What We Actually Deliver — The Weekly Report Model

The core retainer product is a formal weekly report delivered to the executive director and dining leadership. It is not a phone call. It is a document. This distinction matters to operators who answer to ownership groups, boards, and investors — they need something to show.

  • Weekly food cost % vs. budget with variance explanation
  • Labor hours and scheduling efficiency review
  • Vendor invoice spot-check and ordering recommendations
  • Per-plate cost tracking across meal periods
  • 3 action items for the dining director for the coming week
  • Monthly summary report suitable for ownership/investor review

Open Questions & Ideas to Discuss

These are the strategic questions that should be answered before or during Thursday's meeting.

1. Company Name & Brand Identity

The business has no name yet beyond "F&B Senior Living Consulting." A name matters for credibility, LinkedIn, and the capabilities deck going to NIC. What do we want to be called?

2. Who Is the Face of the Business?

Is this Vince solo? A partnership? Mike's restaurant operations background is a clear differentiator — how does his role get positioned on paper and in conversations?

3. The Free Assessment as a Lead Gen Tool

Offer a no-cost "30-minute food cost assessment" to get in the door. Pull their current food cost % and show them the gap in 30 minutes. It's a proven way to earn the paid engagement.

4. What Does the Weekly Report Actually Look Like?

This is the core product. Before the first client, build the template. A polished, branded one-page weekly report is also a sales tool — show it to prospects and let it close for you.

5. Registered Dietitian Partnership

A contracted RD adds clinical credibility for therapeutic diet work without adding payroll. Who in the Birmingham area could fill this role? UAB's dietetics program could be a source.

6. Pilot Client Strategy

Consider a discounted first engagement (e.g., $2,500/mo for 90 days) in exchange for a documented case study and testimonial. One good case study with numbers closes the next 5 clients.

7. Formal Referral Fee Structure

If Nick (Umbredo Living) or others refer clients, is there a referral fee in place? A 10–15% first-year commission is standard. Get this in writing before the first referral happens.

8. The Technology Angle

The dashboard and AI-assisted reporting is a real differentiator — most competitors are spreadsheet-and-phone. How do we productize and show this to prospects? A live demo could be a strong close.

9. SNF Contracted Ops — Is That Year 1 or Year 2?

Full contracted operations (like Morrison) is a bigger lift — staffing, hiring, equipment. Is that a Year 1 pursuit or a Year 2 growth path once consulting revenue is stable? Be honest about capacity.

10. What Happens at the NIC Conference?

Nick can carry our name into the room in Chicago, but what exactly does he hand people? We need a one-page leave-behind with a clear ask by September 15. What's the call to action on that page?
